DESCRIPTION

In 1866, Teradaya was attacked by the famous samurai Sakamoto Ryoma, who helped bring about the end of feudalism in Japan during the Meiji Restauration. The attack is therefore commonly known as the “Teradaya Incident”. Due to its location in Kyoto’s traditional Sake district Fushimi, it is also close to various sights and attractions such as the Fushimi Jugokubune and Gokogu Shrine, making it the perfect addition to any itinerary through the area.
