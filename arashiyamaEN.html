DESCRIPTION

Arashiyama in Kyoto is home to famous attractions such as Tenryuji Temple, the Bamboo Grove Path, and historical and charming architecture, making it one of the top tourist destinations in the ancient capital of Kyoto. Here, we will introduce the Bamboo Grove Path in particular

The Bamboo Grove Path is arguably the most representative location in Arashiyama. You might have seen lush green and mysteriously atmospheric photos of it online, but "seeing is believing." Walking through it gives you a feeling as if you've entered another world. The path is accessible 24 hours a day.

Stretching approximately 600 meters, this path is surrounded by landmarks like the small train station, Arashiyama Station, the famous Zen temple, Tenryuji Temple, and the Okouchi Sansou Garden, which was owned and operated by a prominent actor in the first half of the 20th century. These are located north of the Togetsukyo Bridge. In December, the path is illuminated at night, enhancing its mystical ambiance. During the spring and autumn seasons and around noon during the day, the path can get quite crowded. If you wish to avoid the crowds, it's essential to plan accordingly.
