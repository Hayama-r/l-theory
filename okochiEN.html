DESCRIPTION

Okochi Sanso Garden is located in the Arashiyama-Sagano area of Kyoto, on the south side of Mt. Ogura, which is mentioned in the Hyakunin Isshu, a poem collection which is also subject of a popular pastime during Japanese New Year’s celebrations. Many people just pass by it despite its proximity to the bamboo groves of Arashiyama and Tenryuji Temple, which are well known both in Japan and abroad as tourist destinations. The garden was built in the early 20th century by Denjiro Okochi, a famous actor of period dramas. He continued to design the garden over a span of 30 years from 1931 until his death. The garden is spread over a large area and is characterized by its mountainous landscape and cleverly arranged hills and stones. It is also home to a variety of plants and trees, most notably pine trees, cherry trees, and maples, which allow visitors to enjoy the beauty of nature in every season. There is also an observatory from which you can see almost the entire city of Kyoto from Mt. Hieizan to the south of Kyoto, which is a view not many places can offer. When you have finished strolling through the garden, you can relax with a cup of green tea and sweets with the tea ticket that is included in the entrance fee. When visiting, be sure to wear clothes and shoes appropriate for the season and weather as it is, after all, mostly an outdoor activity.
