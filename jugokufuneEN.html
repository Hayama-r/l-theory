DESCRIPTION

Fushimi Jugokufune are sightseeing boats operated by the Fushimi Tourism Association, a non-profit organization. The boats have a long history and were commonly used to transport sake and rice from Fushimi as well as passengers to and from Osaka from the Edo period (17th century) up until the end of the Meiji era (early 20th century). In 1998, the Fushimi Tourism Association revived them as sightseeing boats. The highlight of Fushimi Jugokufune is that the scenery changes greatly depending on the season.

In spring, the cherry blossoms are in full bloom, and you can fully feel spring.

Enjoying the summer greenery helps ease the heat a bit.
