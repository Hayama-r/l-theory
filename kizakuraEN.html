DESCRIPTION

Kizakura Kappa Country is a famous sake brewery that sells locally brewed sake and beer. There is a store, a café, and a memorial hall where visitors can enjoy sake on site or buy some to take back home. A very large curtain (called “noren”) is hanging at the entrance, making it easy to find.

The museum's store is brimming with all kinds of different sake, and visitors can enjoy looking at the various packages of sake while shopping. (Permission granted to photograph)

On the other side of the building is the Kizakura Memorial Hall, where visitors can learn about the history of Kizakura and the sake brewing process through various displays.

There is also a restaurant where you can enjoy lunch and other drinks.

We purchased these three types here this time. From left,
・Kizakura Soft Highball Umeshu (plum wine): The aroma of plums paired with the fizziness of the soda made this a very pleasant drinking experience.
・The beer had a moderate bitterness with a hint of citrus flavor, making it easy to drink even if you are not a regular beer drinker.
・Kizakura Tsu no Junmai Daiginjo Yamadanishiki (Japanese sake): Although it had a relatively high alcohol content of 15%, the flavor was clear on the nose and very easy to drink.
