DESCRIPTION

There is a heart-shaped window called an "inome window" through which you can enjoy the scenery of the four seasons.

On the ceiling of the room with the heart-shaped window, there are 160 paintings　of flowers and Japanese landscapes.

Upon entering, visitors are given tea sweets, a pamphlet, and a “kano” string.

You can take this “kano” string back with you as a souvenir or tie it to the Jizo Hall on the temple grounds.

Every year during the summer the Furin festival, an event where over 2,000 wind chimes are hung in the main hall, is held, creating a very cool atmosphere even in the hot summer.
