DESCRIPTION

Daigoji Temple was registered as a World Heritage Site in 1994. 6,942 national treasures and 6,521 important cultural properties are in its possession. It is also a very old temple with lots of history.

This is a five-story pagoda built to receive the bodhisattva of Emperor Daigo. A bodhisattva is a person that has not yet attained the necessary enlightenment to become a buddha but is on their way to do so. It may be surprising, but it is the oldest wooden structure in Kyoto.

This is the Karamon gate. As those who are familiar with history may know, it was designed by one of the three “Great Unifiers” of Japan, Toyotomi Hideyoshi himself when he held a cherry blossom viewing party in Daigo. It is designated as a national special historic site and a special place of scenic beauty.

This is the garden of Sanboin, located just behind the Karamon gate mentioned earlier. Most of Sanboin itself is designated as a national treasure, and inside is a statue of a seated Maitreya Bosatsu (Maitreya Bodhisattva), a masterpiece by sculptor Kaikei. (Unfortunately, it is generally not accessible to the public.

Although most people think of Daigoji during the cherry blossom season, the scenery is also very beautiful in the summer. The admission fee is 1000 yen.
