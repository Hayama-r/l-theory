DESCRIPTION

Manpukuji Temple was established in the early Edo period (1603-1867) by Zen master Ingen Ryuichi, who came to Japan from Fujian Province, China, and is the head temple of the Obaku school, one of the three Zen schools of Japan. As you can see from the upwards-slanted eaves on the photo, the architecture is closer to that of China than to that of Japanese temples and shrines

This is the Mampukuji Juto, a hexagonal hall laid with tiles in the hon-gawara style. It is the tomb of and built by Zen Master Ingen Ryuichi during his lifetime and is designated as a National Important Cultural Property.

This is a Chinese mahogany tree which was introduced from China by Zen master Yingyuan Ryuichi for use as a food source. In the back is a Zen dojo. Unfortunately, I was not able to experience it, but you can if you apply in advance.

Here you can also enjoy Fucha cuisine which was brought to Japan from China as Buddhist cuisine and has a tradition spanning more than 300 years. Both flavorful and beautiful to the eye, these meals are available by reservation only

Manpukuji Temple has vast grounds. It you feel like taking a rest, there are several of these resting spaces on the premises.

We hope you will enjoy Manpukuji Temple, especially since it is different from other temples and shrines in Japan and therefore offers a unique experience.Admission is 500 yen.
